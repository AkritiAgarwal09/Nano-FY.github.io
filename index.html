Our Technology




Granular Activated Carbon (GAC) and carbon block


  • Both forms use carbon that has been ground into small particle sizes. The source carbon for GAC is ground to particles that are smaller than 0.84 mm but larger than 0.30 mm.
  • The particles are ground further into “fine mesh” that is 7 to 19 times smaller than GAC (0.045mm~0.18 mm).
  • The uniform pore structure between the individual granules of carbon that allows to achieve better contaminant reduction.
  • increases the contact time with the filter media, further increasing the block’s ability to remove contaminants.
  • It also is used as a treatment method for aesthetic contaminants (removal of taste, odor and color)




Stages of Filtration

  • Stage 1 (sediment filter) removes dust, large particles, and rust.
  • Stage 2 and 3 (carbon block filters) remove chlorine, chemicals, and odor.
  • Stage 3 (RO membrane) removes all particles down to 0.0001 microns (this is really tiny---for some perspective a cross section of human hair is 50 microns). This includes chemicals, salts, heavy metals, and viruses. The water is then stored in a pressurized storage tank, and must pass through the final carbon post-filter before it reaches your faucet.




The TDS controller


The water which comes out of the filter will be 100% which means that the natural minerals required for our body have also been removed. Therefore this water is then passed through a TDS controller. The TDS controller then maintains the natural minerals in the water and sends this water to the UV filter. TDS controller works as a bypass valve which takes a percentage of source water bypassing RO membrane to the output water, which is then filtered through a carbon filter.
